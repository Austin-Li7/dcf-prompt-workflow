# Adobe (ADBE) — Step 3: Competitive Landscape & Porter’s Five Forces
## v5.3 Light Mode validation
## Scope discipline
This Step 3 is intentionally conservative.

- **Company architecture input**: Digital Media, Digital Experience, Publishing and Advertising.
- **Primary-source priority**: Adobe FY2025 10-K; Salesforce SEC / investor filings; HubSpot SEC filings; Shopify financial reports / SEC filings; Microsoft 10-K / investor filings.
- **No hard-coded market share figures** unless directly disclosed by a primary source.
- **No overlabeling**: when the source only shows adjacent competitive scope rather than a direct same-market market-share table, evidence is capped at **STRONG_INFERENCE**, **WEAK_INFERENCE**, or **QUALITATIVE_BASIS** as appropriate.
- **Publishing and Advertising** is treated with extra caution because Adobe itself describes it as a heterogeneous **legacy** bucket rather than a clean modern product market.

## Validation notes

1. **No hard market-share numbers were used** because no primary-source, scope-clean market-share table was identified in the reviewed filings.
2. **No secondary article was used as the core basis** for the final claims.
3. **No full-segment overpairing for Digital Media**: Microsoft was only used confidently for the productivity / consumer-creation adjacency, not as a full clean substitute for Adobe’s entire creative stack.
4. **No forced one-name pairing for Publishing and Advertising**: the segment’s own disclosure is too heterogeneous.
5. **This is Light Mode**: good enough for source-disciplined validation, but not a substitute for a deeper category-by-category Step 3 full-mode run.

# Step 4: Cross-Business Capability Penetration & Flywheel Analysis

## 1. Objective
Identify how the **core capabilities** of one business line act as a 
technology or operational input into another business line's product — 
and trace that penetration to measurable product and financial outcomes.

This is not an analysis of shared infrastructure or resource allocation.
The question is: does Business A's deepest competency show up as a 
differentiated feature or cost advantage inside Business B's product?

## 3. Capability Penetration Framework

### Step 3A — Identify Core Capabilities Per Business Line
For each business line in Step 1, define its **single deepest competency**:
the thing it does that no pure-play competitor in Step 3 can easily replicate.

Examples:
- AI/ML business → real-time inference at scale
- Gaming business → live-service engagement loops
- Cloud business → global low-latency infrastructure

Do not list generic strengths. One capability per business line, 
grounded in what the Step 3 analysis shows competitors cannot match.

### Step 3B — Map Penetration Paths
For each capability identified above, ask:
> Does this capability show up as a feature, cost input, or operational 
> advantage inside any other business line's product?

For each valid penetration path, define:
- **The Capability:** What exactly is being transferred
- **The Mechanism:** How it enters the recipient product 
  (e.g., embedded model, shared API, internal tooling, shared dataset)
- **The Product Impact:** What the recipient product can now do 
  differently or better than its Step 3 pure-play competitor
- **What the Competitor Cannot Do:** Why the Step 3 rival cannot 
  replicate this — structural reason only, not "they're behind"

### Step 3C — Trace to Financial Outcomes
For each penetration path, identify the financial signature in Step 2 data:

| Signal Type | What to Look For |
|:---|:---|
| **Revenue Enablement** | Did the recipient business line accelerate growth after the capability was deployed? |
| **Margin Expansion** | Did gross or operating margin improve in the recipient segment without proportional cost increase? |
| **CAC Reduction** | Is there evidence of faster customer acquisition or higher attach rates in the recipient segment? |
| **Cost Displacement** | Did the recipient segment avoid a CapEx or OpEx line that pure-play competitors must spend on? |

If none of these signals are visible in Step 2 data, the penetration 
path must be marked `product-only` — real but not yet financially 
material.

---

## 4. Flywheel Condition
A penetration path qualifies as a **flywheel** only if it is 
self-reinforcing. Test with this logic:

> Does the recipient business line generate data or scale that 
> flows back to strengthen the source capability?

Example:
> AI capability improves game matchmaking →  
> More players engage longer →  
> More behavioral data flows back to AI models →  
> AI matchmaking improves further

If yes → mark as `flywheel`  
If no → mark as `one-directional penetration`
